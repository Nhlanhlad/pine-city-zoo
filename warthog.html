WARTHOG

Phacochoerus is a genus of wild pigs known as warthogs. They are found in open and semi-open habitats in sub-Saharan Africa, including arid regions. There are two species: the desert warthog and the common warthog.

Warthogs appear mostly hairless from a distance, except for a crest along the back and tufts on the cheeks and tail. The name "warthog" refers to their facial wattles, most prominent in males. They also have long, curved tusks — males’ can reach up to 63.5 cm.

They are largely herbivorous but occasionally eat small animals. The Cape warthog, a subspecies of the desert warthog, became extinct around 1865, but most other warthogs are still common and not considered endangered.
